About

I am a PhD candidate at Institut Jean Nicod, École Normale Supérieure in Paris. My research lies at the intersection of the philosophy of mind, language, and cognitive science.

In my thesis, I investigate how we share representations in linguistic communication. My hypothesis is that communication does not involve the replication of thought from speaker to hearer, but rather a form of coordination of thoughts. My goal is to characterize and explain interpersonal coordination so construed.

Besides my thesis, I am interested in:

  • the nature of human-machine semantic interactions;
  • the role of inner speech in the constitution of thought;
  • the notion of context in communication on social media.
